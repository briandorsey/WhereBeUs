Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does this do, exactly?

A: WhereBeUs lets you see where your friends are on a map. As your friends move, their location is instantly updated.

Q: Wait, which friends can I see?

A: You can see any friends that are also using WhereBeUs.

Q: So how do I tell WhereBeUs who my friends are?

A: It's easy! Just log in with Facebook or Twitter and WhereBeUs will instantly find out who your friends are. Any of your friends that are also using WhereBeUs will instantly show up on the map.

Q: Do I have to keep running WhereBeUs to update my location?

A: Yes. At the moment, if you're "on the move," you should keep WhereBeUs running. You'll disappear from your friends' maps about two hours after your last update with WhereBeUs.

Q: Is there anyway to go "invisible" for a while?

A: There is no explicit way at the moment. But if you don't update with WhereBeUs, after two hours you'll simply disappear.

Q: How does this relate to Foursquare and Gowalla?

A: We think they're complimentary. We think WhereBeUs is most useful when you want to figure out where your friends are right now -- for example, when you're waiting for them at a coffee shop, restaurant, or bar.

Q: How does this relate to Google Latitude?

A: Google Latitude is very similar. What's great about WhereBeUs is you don't have to create a new account or go through the painful process of connecting with your friends. You've already done that with Facebook and Twitter, which is where WhereBeUs gets its information about your friends.

Q: When I send a message, how does that work?

A: If you're using Twitter, your message will be tweeted. If you're using Facebook, your message will appear on your wall. If you're using both, your message will appear in both places.

Q: Who built this?

A: WhereBeUs was written by Dave Peck and Brian Dorsey. We're both software developers hailing from Seattle, WA, USA. Dave is a freelance developer and would be happy to help you build cool new things.

Q: And it's free?

A: Yes, WhereBeUs is free... and it always will be.

Q: Why did you build this?

A: For fun, and because we wanted to make the simplest possible app of this style. Let us know how we did.

Q: And it's open source?

A: Yes, it's open source! We wrote it in just a few weeks, so there's a lot of great stuff we'd love to see added. If you're a developer, we encourage you to make a fork on GitHub and get started building.

Q: What are the next two things you plan to do with WhereBeUs?

A: On the iPhone app, we want to give you a list of all your friends so you can quickly jump between them and can quickly find people who are far away.
On the back-end, we plan to integrate with Twitter and Facebook geo-metadata. What that means is that if your friends don't use WhereBeUs, but they post stuff tagged with a latitude and longitude, they will also automatically show up in your WhereBeUs map.
